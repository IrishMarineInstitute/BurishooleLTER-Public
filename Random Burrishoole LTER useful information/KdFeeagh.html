Depth attenuation coefficient of sunlight ($K_{d}$, $m^{-1}$) on Lough Feeagh.¶

$K_{d}$ can be physically interpreted as how much sunlight 'decays' from its initially surface value as it radiates downward through the water column. The initial surface value is determined by the amount of downwelling sunlight.

'Sunlight' here is in reference to shortwave or solar radiation (i.e. energy directly emitted by the sun) and we are primarily concerned with 'visible' sunlight; that is sunlight in the wavelength band 400-700 nm ($E_{VSR}$). It is this visible component of the sunlight that provides light energy to phytoplankton for photosynthesis and this component of sunlight is referred to as Photosynthetically Active Radiation (PAR). Thus, quantifying the penetration depth of PAR into the water column is important to understanding phytoplankton dynamics.

Factors contributing to the attenuation of $E_{VSR}$ include scattering by particles in the water column (i.e turbidity) and absorption (i.e. by colour). It should be noted that the decay of $E_{VSR}$ with depth increases linearly with colour (so more coloured water absorbs more sunlight closer to the surface) whilst turbidity doesn't actually absorb light but deflects it, meaning that light does not follow a straight path toward the bottom and is scattered in multiple directions.

So how to compute $K_{d}$? The most commonly used method is to relate it to Secchi disk depth ($Z_{SD}$, m). The standard approach in oceanography and limnology uses the value 1.7 for this conversion with 1.7 = $K_{d}$ x $Z_{SD}$. However the value of 1.7 was determined through light attenuation experiments in open ocean conditions and often is not suitable for highly turbid or coloured waters (Feeagh!)

$K_{d}$ can be accurately estimated directly by plotting a profile of light measurements from the surface to below the euphotic zone depth on a logarithmic scale and calculating the exponential decay rate. However such datasets require a light sensor profiler or multiple moored light sensors. A simplistic estimate can be derived by utilising a matched pair of (underwater) light sensors to measure light attenuation over a known depth interval according to the formula:

\begin{equation*} K_{d} = -\frac{d(lnE_{VSR})}{dz} \end{equation*}

Method: On Feeagh 2 HOBO light sensors were installed at 0.5m and 2.0m from October 2014. HOBO sensors measure total luminance (lux). Biel Obrador and Elvira de Eyto ran a trial whereby 4 of these HOBO sensors were deployed next to an underwater Li-Cor PFD sensor on Feeagh (at 0.5 m). The linear relation between the data recorded by each set of sensors allowed the development of a regression relationship to convert lux values recorded by HOBOs to $E_{VSR}$. This conversion allowed the use two estimates of $E_{VSR}$ at depths of 0.5 m and 2.0 m to calculate values of $K_{d}$ using the above equation.

Result: Timeseries of $K_{d}$ for 2014/2015 gave a mean value of 1.4 $m^{-1}$. The reciprocal of $K_{d}$ is the $e$-folding depth $i.e$ the depth at which irradiance has decreased by 1/$e$ (~37%) its initial value. Given the mean $Z_{SD}$ over the same timeframe of 1.8, $K_{d}$ x $Z_{SD}$ = 2.5. Thus, in future a more reasonable estimate of $K_{d}$ from $Z_{SD}$ should be computed using 2.5, in line with the values presented by Koenings and Edmondson (1991) for brown Alaskan lakes. Overall, these results indicates that the euphotic zone depth of Lough Feeagh (1% PAR, ln(100)/$K_{d}$) is only ~ 3.3 m.
